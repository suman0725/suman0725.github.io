Research

Nuclear structure and hadronization with CLAS12.

My work uses semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering to study how quarks and hadrons behave inside nuclei and how the nuclear environment modifies measured observables.

Semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering

In SIDIS, an incoming electron scatters from a target and one or more final-state hadrons are detected. The electron determines the deep-inelastic scattering kinematics, while the detected hadron gives access to transverse momentum, fragmentation, and azimuthal structure.

Nuclear TMD observables

My analysis focuses on nuclear modifications of hadron production. Important observables include multiplicity ratios, transverse-momentum broadening, and unpolarized azimuthal moments.

Targets and detector

The analysis uses CLAS12 Run Group D data from deuterium, carbon, copper, and tin targets. Charged-particle tracking, time-of-flight information, Cherenkov detectors, and detector-quality selections are combined to identify electron and pion candidates.

Analysis priorities

  • Reliable event and particle selection
  • Detector acceptance and efficiency studies
  • Monte Carlo validation
  • Multiplicity-ratio extraction
  • Transverse-momentum broadening
  • Azimuthal distributions and moments
