Experimental Nuclear Physics

From CLAS12 detector data to nuclear-structure observables.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Physics at Temple University. My research focuses on semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, nuclear transverse-momentum-dependent distributions, hadronization, detector studies, and scientific software for CLAS12.

What I work on

Nuclear SIDIS

Studying semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering from deuterium, carbon, copper, and tin targets with CLAS12.

Nuclear TMDs

Investigating transverse momentum, nuclear modifications, multiplicity ratios, and azimuthal observables.

Scientific Computing

Building reproducible data-processing, Monte Carlo, detector-calibration, and analysis workflows in C++, ROOT, Python, and Git.
